Key Differences

  • Abstraction: The OSI model is a generic, conceptual model intended to describe all forms of network communication. TCP/IP is a practical model designed around the specific protocols that make up the internet.
  • Combined Layers: TCP/IP combines the OSI's Application, Presentation, and Session layers into a single Application layer, treating formatting and session management as part of the application's responsibility.
  • Lower Layers: TCP/IP often groups the Data Link and Physical layers into a single Network Access (or Link) layer, focusing on how IP packets are framed and transmitted on the local network.
  • Development: TCP/IP was developed by the Department of Defense (DoD) before the OSI model. As a result, the TCP/IP protocols were already widely adopted by the time the OSI model was formalized.
